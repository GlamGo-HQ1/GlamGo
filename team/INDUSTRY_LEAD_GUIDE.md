# GlamGo — Industry Specialist Onboarding
*Role: Product Manager & Legal/Domain Expert*

Welcome to the team! Since you are both a **Lawyer and a Hairstylist**, you are the "secret weapon" for this hackathon. You do not need to write a single line of code. Instead, your job is to make sure the app feels authentic, looks professional to real stylists, and protects itself legally from bad actors.

Here is your exact Task List for the 4-day sprint:

---

## 🟡 Task 1: The Legal & Policy Framework
*Because we are holding client money in escrow and taking commissions, we need strict, legally sound rules.*

1. **The Cancellation Policy:** Write the final draft of the cancellation tiers. Example: "Cancellations under 12 hours incur a 50% penalty to compensate the stylist." Write the exact wording we will show the users.
2. **The "Dispute" Resolution Rules:** If a stylist says "I did the hair" but the client refuses to hand over the 4-digit confirmation code, what is our legal/business policy? How do we resolve it?
3. **Terms of Service (Short Version):** Write a 3-paragraph "Terms of Service" that users must accept. It needs to cover that we are a *marketplace connector*, not an employer of the stylists, to limit our liability.

## 🟢 Task 2: The "Seed Data" (The Authenticity Check)
*Developers use fake, ugly data (like "Test Hair 1 - $10"). We need you to curate the database so it looks like a premium, live application when the judges view the gallery.*

1. **The Core 20 Hairstyles:** Create a list of 20 high-end hairstyles (e.g., Knotless Braids, Frontal Installations). 
2. **Pricing & Timing:** For every hairstyle, write down the realistic baseline price (in Naira) and exactly how many hours it takes to complete.
3. **Descriptions:** Write a one-sentence premium description for each style (e.g., "A sleek, low-tension protective style perfect for natural hair retention.")

*Beloved will give you a simple Google Sheet or Word Doc. Just fill it out, and the developers will inject it into the database.*

## 🔵 Task 3: The "Idiot Test" (UX Audit)
*Developers design things for other developers. You must design them for standard users.*

1. **Review the Booking Flow:** When Beloved builds the first version of the booking screen, he will hand you his phone. 
2. **The Goal:** Can a 45-year-old hairstylist who is bad at technology figure out how to accept a booking and get paid?
3. **Feedback:** Your job is to rip apart anything that is confusing. "This button is too small." "A stylist wouldn't know what 'Escrow' means, change the text to 'Funds Secured'."

---
**Your output is the difference between a random coding project and a professional startup.**
