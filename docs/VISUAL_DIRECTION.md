# GlamGo — Visual Direction

**Decision 7 from PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md — RESOLVED**
**Direction chosen:** Dark Luxury Editorial

---

## The Vibe in One Sentence

GlamGo looks like a high-end fashion magazine had a baby with a luxury car configurator — dark, cinematic, editorial, and unmistakably premium.

---

## What It IS

- A dark interface where hairstyles are the stars — lit dramatically against deep, rich backgrounds
- Typography-driven like Vogue — bold serif headlines, clean sans-serif body text
- Glassmorphic panels that float over gradient mesh backgrounds
- Subtle motion everywhere: cards breathe, transitions flow, nothing is static
- Cinematic, futuristic, and unapologetically luxury

## What It is NOT

- ❌ Bright pastel "beauty app" aesthetic (too generic)
- ❌ Pinterest-style flat image grid (too disposable)
- ❌ White minimalist tech product (too cold)
- ❌ Instagram-clone social feed (too casual)
- ❌ Cluttered with icons, badges, and decorations (too noisy)

## Key Visual Techniques

1. **Film grain overlay** — SVG noise texture at 3% opacity over hero sections. Adds editorial magazine feel.
2. **Gradient mesh backgrounds** — Dark amorphous color fields (purple → navy) behind hero and gallery sections.
3. **Glassmorphism** — Semi-transparent panels with backdrop blur over gradient backgrounds.
4. **Ken Burns motion** — Subtle zoom/pan animation on all gallery card images. Nothing is static.
5. **Dark gradient overlay on images** — Bottom 40% of every hairstyle image has a dark gradient for text readability.
6. **High-contrast photography** — All hairstyle photos shot against dark backgrounds, warm color-graded.

## The Emotional Test

When a woman opens GlamGo, she should feel:
- "This is serious" — not another basic app
- "These hairstyles look expensive" — even if the prices are affordable
- "I want to keep scrolling" — the presentation creates desire
- "This is different from anything I've seen" — cinematic, not social media

---

## Why This Direction

1. **Dark makes hair pop** — Braids, locs, weaves, and natural textures look dramatically better against dark backgrounds than against white
2. **Luxury signals premium** — Users perceive services as higher-quality when the platform feels expensive
3. **Judges notice** — Dark luxury with motion is far more visually impressive than a clean white app
4. **Performance-friendly** — Dark UIs use less battery (OLED screens), important for the Nigerian market
5. **Research consensus** — All 4 independent research reports recommended this direction
